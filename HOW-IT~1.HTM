End-to-End Process

How CADRIA works

From an AI agent's raw output to a fully audited, risk-bounded decision — in seven steps.

1

AI agent produces an output

A capable AI system acts autonomously — generating content, decisions, plans, or actions across any domain or modality.

2

System extracts verifiable claims

The output is decomposed into discrete, verifiable claims, steps, or assertions. Each claim becomes an independent unit of review, making even complex outputs tractable for oversight.

3

Claims are routed to relevant HDRs

Claims are sent to a dynamically selected network of Human Digital Representatives with the right domain expertise:

  • Legal — regulatory and compliance review
  • Medical — health and clinical safety
  • Ethics — fairness, values, and societal impact
  • Engineering — technical correctness and feasibility
  • Finance — economic and fiduciary risk
  • Safety — direct and indirect harm assessment

Selection is randomised to resist strategic pressure, and routing adapts to risk and context.

4

HDRs evaluate and provide scores & evidence

Each HDR evaluates its assigned claims and returns scores, supporting evidence, and rationale — creating a rich, multi-perspective assessment rather than a single verdict.

5

Consensus engine aggregates results

The consensus engine combines HDR assessments into a consensus score with probabilistic confidence, and calculates worst-case risk bounds (low / medium / high) for the output as a whole.

6

Control decision is taken

Based on consensus and risk bounds, the system gates the output in real time:

Approve — output released Revise — returned to the AI agent with feedback Reject / Escalate — blocked and escalated to human experts Human Review — high-risk cases reviewed by humans
7

Outcomes are logged and feed back into the system

Every action is recorded in an audit trail. Real-world outcomes — including delayed or indirect harms detected through long-horizon monitoring — improve the HDRs, consensus models, thresholds, and routing for every future review.
